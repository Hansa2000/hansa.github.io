About Me

I am an electronics engineer who enjoys working on multidisciplinary projects and who has a strong theoretical background in biomedical instrumentation, analog electronics, computer architecture, and signal processing. My research experience spans across a few domains, including human-computer interaction (HCI) and haptic feedback systems, 3D printing, wearable devices, and embedded systems. Additionally, I have worked on projects involving analog and mixed-signal IC design, as well as hardware acceleration. Currently I'm working as an embedded systems engineer at Thakshana Technologies Pvt. Ltd. in Sri Lanka.

Research

3D-Printed Flexible EEG Electrodes for Neonatal Monitoring

As part of the Braineocare25 project, we developed flexible, fully 3D-printed EEG electrodes suitable for neonatal monitoring. Our innovative design successfully captured high-quality EEG signals from team members (adults), validating its potential for non-invasive, comfortable monitoring in vulnerable infants. We hope this advancement paves the way for safer neurodiagnostic tools in NICUs.

Flexible Electrodes 3D-printing Neonatal Care

Integrating Force Sensing with Electro-Tactile Feedback

We developed a novel 3D-printed electro-tactile interface that simultaneously senses force and delivers tactile feedback, eliminating the need for separate sensors. Validated through a user study (N=10), the approach simplifies design complexity while maintaining flexibility—opening new possibilities for intuitive, sensor-free tactile interfaces in wearables.

Projects

A Fractional-N Phase Locked Loop (PLL) Clock Multiplier in IHP SG13G2 PDK

We designed a fractional-N PLL using the IHP SG13G2 PDK, targeting output frequencies from 10 kHz to 150 MHz with a 10 MHz reference input. This project was developed using open-source analog IC design tools. The goal was to gain hands-on experience with analog mixed-signal design flow while contributing to open-source EDA ecosystems. This is currently an ongoing project, and we are planning to submit for Tiny Tapeout.

RFIC PLL IHP SG13G2

Neonatal seizure prediction system (Braineocare 25)

For our final year project, we developed a neonatal seizure prediction system. It captures EEG and ECG signals from infants and feeds those to a machine learning model to predict seizures. We have validated the performance of the hardware system using simultaneously captured EEG signals from our device and the hospital's EEG device. And performance of the machine learning model using public datasets.

Fitness Rubber Band Monitor

The Fitness Rubber Band Monitor is a wearable device designed to track and analyze resistance band exercises. Using sensitive strain gauge sensors integrated into the rubber band, the system detects resistance variations during stretching.

Design and Implementation of a Custom System Bus

Designed and implemented a system bus on FPGA, inspired by AMBA 2 specifications. Successfully developed split-transaction capable slaves and arbiters, verified through simulation. This project helped us to gain skills in bus protocols, FPGA programming, and arbitration techniques for efficient data transfer.
